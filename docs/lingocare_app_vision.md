## 1. What Lingocare Is

Lingocare is a **personalized education platform** that simultaneously relieves and empowers teachers while providing every trainee with a personalized AI tutor — one that meets them exactly where they are. It is designed for **everyone in Germany who wants to become a care worker**: international nursing trainees and domestic training programmes alike, regardless of background or country of origin. As a platform, Lingocare is **AI-native and content-ready** — the only system built specifically to train care workers in both nursing knowledge and clinical German simultaneously, in context, and adapted to each learner.

**AI-native** means the intelligence is not a feature layer added to an existing LMS. It is the foundation. Content adapts to each learner's language level. Exercises are evaluated by AI. The teaching assistant knows the curriculum, the class, and the individual learner. The platform learns from every cohort to improve for the next one.

**Content-ready** means a school does not arrive at a blank platform. When a school signs up, the nursing curriculum content is already there — Learning Units, Knowledge Building Blocks, exercise sets, Language Garden content — aligned to the Hessen Pflegefachassistenz framework. It is plug-and-play. Schools get running in a week, not a semester.

The **Content Studio** allows schools to go beyond the pre-built content. Instructors and content leads can create or edit any Practice Focus, Knowledge Building Block, or Expert Mode scenario. Magic Write — the AI drafting tool — generates a first version in seconds, which humans then review and publish. The pre-built content is the starting point. The studio is the path forward.

It is not a language app with nursing content bolted on. It is not an LMS that happens to have some German exercises. It is one integrated system where language and professional knowledge are inseparable — because in real clinical practice they are.

**The one-sentence version:** Lingocare helps nursing trainees in Germany — international and domestic alike — finish their training and become qualified care workers, by making clinical German learnable through care itself and providing a personalized AI tutor at every step, while giving their school the visibility and tools to support the journey.

**What makes it different from everything else:**

- Language is taught through clinical scenarios, not isolated grammar drills — the content IS the language training
- Every piece of content is curriculum-aligned (starting with Hessen) and ready to use from day one
- The AI adapts content to each learner's mother tongue, language level, and learning history
- All three client roles — Tenant Director, School Admin, Instructor, Learner — are served by one connected system with a shared AI layer (Ask Lingo) across every dashboard
- Every feature is tested in a real classroom before it ships (Class-First AI)
- Schools can extend and customise content through the built-in studio without needing external tools

## 2. The Problem We Exist to Solve

### The Numbers

Across Europe, more than **4 million healthcare specialists** are currently missing — a structural and growing deficit. Germany's share of this crisis:

Germany needs approximately **500,000 more nurses by 2030**. The existing training pipeline cannot fill this gap:

- Roughly **57,000** trainees begin nursing programmes each year across approximately **1,500 Pflegeschulen**
- Annual demand (~75,000 qualified nurses/year) already outpaces graduates (~50,000/year)
- The projected shortfall could reach **690,000 by 2049** if structural problems are not addressed

**1 in 4 trainees drops out before completing their programme.** Germany is not just failing to train enough nurses — it is losing a significant share of the ones already in training.

### Why Trainees Drop Out

Dropout has four root causes:
**1. Language barriers, especially among international trainees**
Between 30–50% of trainees at many Pflegeschulen are international students, many arriving with A2–B1 German proficiency. Clinical nursing requires B2+ — precise, context-specific language for patient communication, documentation, and team coordination. No tool teaches care German in the way it is actually used: embedded in clinical situations, not in a textbook exercise.

**2. Generic, non-adaptive learning materials**
Most Pflegeschulen use printed materials and generic lessons that were not written for a trainee learning in their second or third language. The same content goes to every student regardless of background, language level, or prior knowledge.

**3. No real-time support or visibility**
Trainees hit a wall — in a clinical placement, in a difficult topic, in a moment of language confusion — and there is no one to help. Teachers do not know who is struggling until it is too late. Directors discover a class is behind by weeks only after the term has moved on.

**4. Generic AI tools filling the gap — without pedagogical purpose**
More than three-quarters of nursing trainees already use AI in their studies. But the tools they reach for are generic large language models — not trained as educators, not specialised for nursing, not designed for professional language development. They give answers. They do not teach. The problem is not that trainees use AI; the problem is that the AI available to them has no pedagogical foundation, no nursing specialisation, and no accountability for learning outcomes.

### Why Schools Struggle

- Teachers spend 60%+ of preparation time creating materials from scratch, without AI support
- Small Pflegeschulen lack the digital infrastructure of large hospital-affiliated schools
- Curriculum compliance is managed manually — spreadsheets, paper calendars, email
- There is no dashboard that tells a director at a glance which classes are on track, which teachers are overloaded, and which students are about to leave

- Many nursing instructors are themselves early in their teaching careers — young educators still developing their own practice who need professional support and mentoring alongside their students

## 3. Problems by User — Jobs to Be Done

### 3.1 The Learner

The learner is the centre of everything. Every other role exists to serve their learning outcome.

**Who they are:** Nursing trainees (Pflegefachassistenz) — both international and domestic. A representative learner: **Lena**, 25, who has recently started her nursing training and wants to pass her theoretical and practical exams while building the confidence to communicate safely and effectively in clinical German every day. Age range typically 18–35. Many international trainees arrive with A2–B1 German. High motivation to qualify, but facing a genuinely hard challenge: learning a technically demanding profession in a second or third language, often far from home.

| Job | Current reality without Lingocare |
|---|---|
| I need to understand clinical German in context, not in isolation | Textbooks are in formal German. Colleagues are busy. No one to ask. |
| I need content at my language level — not too hard, not condescending | Same materials for everyone. No acknowledgement I am learning in a second language. |
| I need to know where I stand and what comes next | No personal dashboard. Progress is invisible. Motivation fades. |
| I need to practise clinical communication, not just read theory | Nothing practice-based. No simulated conversations. No scenarios before placement. |
| I need to stay engaged across a long training programme | No gamification. No sense of momentum. No community within the class. |
| I need help available outside of class hours | Teacher only reachable in school. Nothing available at 10pm when I am stuck. |
| I need to understand the theory behind what I do in practice | Textbooks are separate from exercises. Theory and action are disconnected. |

**Their emotional reality:** Many international trainees describe being competent in care tasks but voiceless in German. Their skills are real but cannot be expressed. Lingocare addresses this specific pain — not just language learning in the abstract, but restoring professional confidence.

## 4. How Lingocare AI Solves Each Problem

Each user — learner, instructor, or director — receives their own **personalized Lingocare AI assistant**, purpose-built for their role and context. Across every dashboard, Lingocare AI:
- Takes over **cognitive load** so each user can focus on what matters most
- **Proactively prepares** each user for what lies ahead — that day, that week, that block
- Supports daily work in the moment — not just when asked, but anticipating needs before they arise
- **Learns over time**: because Lingocare AI operates within the full system context — curriculum, content, individual history — it becomes more tailored and useful the longer it is used

### 4.1 For the Learner

**"I need content at my language level"**
→ At onboarding, each learner sets their German language level (L1 / L2 / L3 — low, intermediate, high). All content in the platform is displayed at their chosen level. The case texts, instructions, and feedback all adapt. A learner at L1 sees simpler clinical German; an L3 learner sees full clinical vocabulary. This is always active — the learner never has to request it.

**"I need clinical German in context, not grammar drills"**
→ Every Learning Unit begins with a clinical case (never theory first). Language emerges from the situation. During a Lesson, the platform surfaces connections to specific Language Garden exercises when a linguistic element is particularly relevant to the topic — not interrupting the flow, but making the connection visible.

**"I need to know where I stand"**
→ XP, streaks, level progression, and a class leaderboard make visible what is usually invisible in nursing training: momentum. The personal dashboard shows today's learning plan, progress by block, achievements, and what is recommended next. The learner always knows where they are.

**"I need help when I am stuck"**
→ The Adaptive Referral engine monitors when a learner is consistently failing on a specific task type. It proactively suggests the Language Garden when the stumbling block appears linguistic, or a relevant Knowledge Building Block when the issue is conceptual. The learner is never left alone with confusion.

**"I need to practise clinical communication"**
→ The Language Garden provides dedicated clinical German practice — separate from content tasks, always accessible, recommended proactively by Lingo Otter. PF3 exercises (Decision Simulation, 6-node) put the learner in the role of the nursing care assistant making real decisions with a persistent risk bar showing the consequences of each choice.

---

### 4.2 For the Instructor

**"I need to prepare materials faster"**
→ Pre-built curriculum-aligned content is already available when the school joins. For anything beyond the standard content, Magic Write generates a first draft — an exercise set, a Knowledge Building Block, a PF task — from a topic description in seconds. Instructors review, adjust, and publish. They are editors, not authors starting from blank.

**"I need real-time class insight"**
→ The Class Analytics view shows submission status across all students, Decision Score vs Activation Score per learner, and automatic flagging of students performing weakly. The Answer Distribution view shows exactly which answers students chose — and where the class is confused as a group, not just individually.

**"I need to spot who is about to drop out"**
→ The Alert system surfaces specific students flagged by name: inactive for N days, consistently below threshold, not submitted. One click sends a check-in message through the platform.

**"I need to run simulation exercises without building them from scratch"**
→ PF3 exercises (Decision Simulation, 6-node) and the full exercise format library are available in the Content Studio. The instructor configures the scenario details; the BB-11 (Branching Decision Simulation) engine handles the logic — persistent risk bar, 6 decision nodes, debriefing report.

**"I need an AI assistant that knows my context"**
→ Ask Lingo is present on the Instructor dashboard. It knows the curriculum, the class roster, the calendar, and the content library. Every dashboard role has access to Ask Lingo — the intelligence is not siloed to one user type.

---

### 4.3 For the School Admin

**"I need the platform to be ready from day one"**
→ Content-ready means the school arrives to a platform that already has the Hessen nursing curriculum mapped, the Learning Units written, and the exercise sets built. Setup is about configuring their term structure, activating their modules, and inviting their users — not building content from scratch.

**"I need to know if we are on track"**
→ The Director Dashboard shows a Gantt-style curriculum schedule across all classes, colour-coded by progress status (on track / at risk / delayed), with progress bars that auto-update from learner activity. A director answers "how is the school doing this week?" in under 30 seconds.

**"I need to see the curriculum in different ways"**
→ The curriculum can be viewed in three dimensions: Blockwise (the default — Block 1, 2, 3 across the term), by Action Competence Cluster (AD / KI / BD / LP grouping), or through Custom Tags defined by the school. The director sees what they need in the format that makes sense for their school.

---

### 4.4 For the Tenant Director

**"I need one view across all my schools"**
→ Tenant-level analytics aggregate performance across schools. The Tenant Director does not need to log into each school individually. Systemic issues (all schools behind on the same topic) are visible at the tenant level.

**"I need consistent standards without micromanaging"**
→ Content published at the tenant level is available to all schools under the tenant. Individual School Admins can supplement but the tenant-level baseline is always present.

## 5. The Seven Roles — Who Uses This

The permission architecture is **tenant-first, school-second.** A tenant (for example, Pflegeakademie Datteln as an organisation) can run multiple schools. Permissions enforce downward only — invitations always flow from higher roles to lower.

```
LINGOCARE INTERNAL              TENANT SIDE (Client-Facing)
──────────────────────────────  ─────────────────────────────────────────
● Super Admin                   ● Tenant Director
  Platform · all tenants          All schools under the tenant
  Full control                    
                                ○ School Admin
○ Admin                           Single school scope
  Platform · most operations      All programmes, all teachers, all learners
                                
○ Sales                         ○ Instructor
  Read-only · business             Assigned programmes
  analytics                        Enrolled learners only
                                
                                ○ Learner
                                  Own enrolments, own calendar, own data
```

**Invitation hierarchy (tenant side):** Tenant Director → School Admin → Instructor → Learner. Each role can only invite the roles below it.

**Ask Lingo is present on all four client-side dashboards** (Tenant Director, School Admin, Instructor, Learner). The questions each role can ask differ based on their data scope, but the AI assistant is never absent from any user's experience.

**Platform enforcement:** All permissions are enforced at the model layer using `tenant_id` and `school_id`. No client-side enforcement — every API endpoint validates the requesting user's role before returning data.

## 6. How the Application Feels

### Design Principles

**Warm, not clinical.** Lingocare orange (`#ED7D00`) and cream (`#FAF7F2`) — not the sterile blue-grey of most EdTech. It feels like the kind of classroom where learning actually happens.

**Clear, not simple.** Complex curriculum data (Gantt charts, progress bars, alert panels) is made readable without being dumbed down. Professionals see professional-grade information.

**Encouraging, not gamified for its own sake.** XP and streaks map to real learning milestones, not arbitrary points. Lingo the otter 🦦 is the platform's friendly face — present at key moments, never intrusive.

**Adaptive, not uniform.** The same topic looks different depending on who is reading it and at what language level. The platform knows who you are before you tell it anything today.

**AI everywhere, invisibly.** Ask Lingo is not a feature the user navigates to — it is present on every dashboard. The adaptive referral is not a pop-up flow — it is a quiet suggestion in the right moment. AI is the infrastructure, not the interface.

### How Lingocare AI Communicates

These principles govern every AI-generated message, proactive nudge, dashboard suggestion, and adaptive referral — across all user types and dashboards:

**Proactive, not reactive.** Lingocare AI takes initiative. It does not wait to be asked. It anticipates what each user needs based on curriculum state, the calendar, and individual history — and surfaces it before the user has to search for it.

**Strength-oriented, not deficit-focused.** Lingocare AI always builds from what is present and what is working. It never points out what is missing, what has not been done, or what has been neglected. Every interaction starts from strength and builds forward from there.

**Human language, not AI language.** In every dashboard interaction and proactive message, Lingocare AI speaks like a warm, encouraging human colleague — not a chatbot. Phrases such as "You haven't logged in for X days" or any reference to gaps, absences, or inactivity are strictly prohibited. What is said matters. How it is said matters equally.

**Motivating and supportive.** Every AI-generated message should feel like a quiet, confident voice that says: *you've got this, and here's what's next.* The tone is never urgent, never nagging, never clinical — always forward-looking and affirming.

## 7. The Content Architecture — What the App Is Built On

Lingocare is built on a proprietary pedagogical framework co-developed with Petra Rahn (Co-Founder, nursing educator). Everything in the platform — every screen, every exercise, every AI output — flows from this framework. Understanding it is essential to understanding what the app does.

---

### 7.1 The Three Levels of Nursing Knowledge

This framework is the analytical foundation of all content in Lingocare. It is not a UI pattern — it is how content is classified and designed.

| Level | Name | What it contains | Where it appears in the app |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | Theory | Laws, guidelines, scientific taxonomy — the "why" behind nursing rules | Knowledge Building Blocks |
| **Level 2** | Subject Logic | Clinical reasoning, decision trees, indication criteria — the principles | Knowledge Building Blocks + situational LU context |
| **Level 3** | Action | Exact process steps — the concrete "how to do it" | Learning Units (primary content) |

Learning Units are **Level 3 primary, Level 2 situational** — they teach through doing, and clinical reasoning appears only when the case demands it, never as abstract theory. Knowledge Building Blocks cover **Level 1 + generalising Level 2** — they are the reusable theoretical foundation that lives in the knowledge library and surfaces when a learner needs to understand the "why."

---

### 7.2 The Curriculum Hierarchy

This is how a nursing programme is structured inside Lingocare, as the user sees it:

```
Module (Block 1, Block 2, Block 3…)
  Duration: month range (e.g. Block 1 runs across months 1–3)
  └── Topic (Themenfeld)
        Duration: date range within the block
        └── Lesson
              Duration: specific dates (one or more class sessions)
              ├── Learning Units (LUs)
              │     → Each LU contains exactly 4 Practice Focuses (PF1–PF4)
              │     → LUs reference Knowledge Building Blocks (optional, non-mandatory)
              ├── Lab Lessons
              │     → Practical skill sessions — no PFs, no exercises
              │     → Scheduled alongside LUs to mark hands-on practice time
              └── Expert Mode (Scenario)
                    → 3–4 scenarios placed in the final blocks of the programme
                    → Each scenario is ~4 hours — a full clinical simulation session
                    → Expert Mode is the highest level of integration: combines LU content,
                       language, and clinical decision-making in one extended scenario
```

A concrete example for a 12-month APH course: approximately 6 Blocks, each with 3–4 Topics, each Topic containing multiple Lessons with LUs, Lab Lessons, and Expert Mode scenarios in the final blocks.

---

### 7.3 The Three Curriculum Dimensions

The curriculum can be viewed and filtered along three dimensions — content creators and directors can switch between them without changing the underlying data:

**1. Blockwise (default)**
The primary view: Block 1 → Block 2 → Block 3 in chronological order. This is how most directors and instructors think about the term — "what are we covering in Block 3?"

**2. Action Competence Clusters (Kompetenzcluster)**
The same content re-viewed through the lens of nursing competency domains:
- **AD** — Work & Implementation (procedural knowledge: step-by-step, practical execution)
- **KI** — Communication & Interaction (relational + communicative competence)
- **BD** — Observation & Documentation (perceptual + clinical reasoning)
- **LP** — Professional Identity & Self-Care (ethics, reflection — planned, not in MVP)

**3. Custom Tags**
Schools can define their own tags for any curriculum dimension that matters to them — regional requirements, clinical placement mapping, assessment groupings. Custom Tags add flexibility without breaking the standard structure.

---

### 7.4 The Four Practice Focuses (PF1–PF4)

Every Learning Unit contains exactly four Practice Focuses. They are **not sequential** — learners can access them in any order. Each PF represents a different cognitive depth applied to the same clinical content. No global "level" is assigned per learner — PF state is stored per learner × LU, so each learner has an independent path through each unit.

**All PFs share:**
- Required material: full case text + airlock diagram (must be read before tasks)
- Opening: agenda, progress bar, target time, breadcrumbs
- Closing: learner-facing message + optional Knowledge Building Block connections (collapsible)
- Teacher dashboard (content varies per PF)

**PF1 — Foundations (Remember · Understand)**
Entry level. No prior knowledge needed. The clinical case introduces vocabulary and core concepts. Exercise formats: Hotspot Marking, Label-It, Drag & Drop Sorting, Sequence Reconstruction, Emotion Barometer, Brainstorming / Free Association.

**PF2 — Analysis (Apply · Analyse)**
Theory-heavy. Exam preparation level. The learner applies reasoning to variations on the scenario — what went wrong, what should have happened, which factor to address. Exercise formats: Annotated Error Analysis, Structured Mapping, Root Cause Analysis, Chip Mechanics (Categorise / Rank / Position), Timed challenges (Error Bingo, Detective Mode, Domino Chain, Risk Meter, Before/After), Justified Selection, Target–Actual Comparison, Collaborative Discussion.

**PF3 — Simulation (Evaluate · Decide)**
Step-by-step decision making. The learner takes the role of the nursing care assistant and works through **6 sequential decision nodes** in a clinical scenario. A persistent risk bar tracks the consequences of each decision. After all 6 nodes, a debriefing report is generated. PF3 uses BB-11 (Branching Decision Simulation) — one signature format, always the same structure.

**PF4 — Reflection (Professional · Ethical · Institutional)**
The learner reflects at the professional level — ethics, institutional context, team dynamics, patient perspective. This is not about correct answers but about informed professional positioning. Exercise formats: Tension Mapping, Team Conversation, Position Barometer, Error Reflection (3-step prompt), Collaborative Discussion, Resident Perspective, Before/After (Growth).

---

### 7.5 Knowledge Building Blocks (KBBs)

Knowledge Building Blocks are structured sequences of theoretical knowledge — the "why" behind nursing actions. They are the Level 1 + Level 2 layer of the content system.

**Structure:** Card → Quiz → Card → Quiz → Card → Quiz (repeating). One concrete knowledge element per block. 2–6 cards per block. Maximum duration: **5 minutes**. Each card contains text + image (or diagram). Each quiz is MC / True-False / Multi-Select — clear right/wrong.

**Availability:**
- During a PF task: offered as an optional hint. The learner can open it or ignore it — no interruption to the exercise flow
- End of a PF: suggested as optional deepening — "want to understand the theory behind this?"
- Anytime: accessible from the Knowledge Library at any point, independently, without needing to be in an LU
- System-triggered: if a learner repeatedly struggles with a concept, the system proactively suggests the relevant KBB

**Key characteristic:** One KBB is **reusable across multiple LUs and contexts**. A KBB on "Infection chain basics" is linked from AD1 (Hygiene) and from KI1 (Communication) wherever that concept is relevant. Content creators build once, reference everywhere.

---

### 7.6 Language Gardens

Language Gardens are dedicated clinical German practice spaces — one per active Mega Cluster. They exist **separately from the Learning Unit flow** and are always accessible as a standalone area. They are not embedded inside every LU — they are a parallel track.

**Language level scope:** Language Gardens are **active for L1 and L2 learners by default**. L3 learners can toggle the Language Garden on if they want dedicated language practice, but it is not recommended proactively at their level.

**During a Lesson:** The platform surfaces language-to-content connections — a short note alongside a specific LU element that points to a relevant Language Garden exercise. Not a redirect — a suggestion. The learner stays in the LU flow.

**In the Language Garden:** Lingo Otter 🦦 greets the learner with a personalised summary of their language progress and a direct suggestion of what to practise next — "You've been working on Hygiene this week. Your Grammatik plant for AD could use some watering." The Otter connects language practice to the nursing content the learner is actually doing.

**Each Language Garden trains the dominant speech act of its Mega Cluster:**

| Cluster | Dominant Speech Act | Language Garden Focus |
|---|---|---|
| AD | Anleiten & Erklären | Explaining procedures step by step |
| KI | Empathisch sprechen | Expressing empathy and building trust |
| BD | Beobachten & Beschreiben | Describing clinical observations precisely |
| LP | Reflektieren & Positionieren | Professional self-reflection (post-MVP) |

**Four micro-skill areas (same structure in every garden, cluster-specific content):**

1. **Grammatik** — Signature grammar for the cluster's dominant speech act. Fixed per garden, defined by content creator.
2. **Hören & Aussprechen** — Listening comprehension and pronunciation using authentic nursing language — the sounds and rhythms of real clinical speech.
3. **Wortschatz & Redemittel** — Vocabulary and set phrases needed for the cluster's dominant speech act (e.g., explaining a procedure, expressing empathy).
4. **Mini-Produktion** — Short guided production tasks. Learners actively use the language they have been practising. The bridge from receptive to productive competence.

**Language Garden exercise formats (SG series):** Listen & Identify, Listen & Sequence, Listen & Select, Dictation-Light, Listen & Repeat, Verbal Sequencing.

---

### 7.7 Expert Mode (Scenarios)

Expert Mode is the highest level of integration in the Lingocare system. It combines nursing knowledge, language competence, and clinical decision-making into one extended simulation.

- **Placement:** 3–4 Expert Mode scenarios, placed in the final Blocks of the programme
- **Duration:** approximately 4 hours per scenario — a full class session
- **Format:** A complex clinical case with multiple phases, drawing on content from across the term's Mega Clusters
- **References:** Expert Mode scenarios reference Practice Focuses (drawing on the PF3 simulation structure), Knowledge Building Blocks, and Language Garden content
- **MVP status:** Expert Mode is on the roadmap. It is not in the Phase 1 MVP but is a committed part of the content plan.

---

### 7.8 The AI Pedagogical Parameters (Non-Negotiable)

Every piece of content generated or surfaced by Lingocare AI — regardless of topic, user, or context — follows these rules. They are hardcoded into every generation template:

1. **Expert mentor tone.** Empathetic, congruent, appreciative. Experienced colleague, not grading machine. Never a textbook. Never a chatbot.
2. **Situation before definition.** Clinical case first. Theory second. Always. Prompt hypothesis formation first, then reveal theory.
3. **Uncertainty as professionalism.** Nursing knowledge is incomplete. The AI models this — refers to real experts (hygiene specialists, medical staff) when in doubt.
4. **Strict prohibitions.** No medical diagnoses. No actions without consent. No leaving vulnerable patients unattended. No invented diagnostic terms.
5. **Mandatory disclosures.** Hand disinfection. Privacy protection. Activation principle (as much help as necessary, as much self-care as possible). Documentation of patient reaction.

### 8.7 The Content Studio

The Content Studio is the creation and editing environment for all platform content. It is accessible to Instructors, School Admins, and Lingocare's own content team.

**What can be created or edited:**
- Practice Focuses (PF1–PF4 exercise sets for any Learning Unit)
- Knowledge Building Blocks (Card+Quiz sequences, up to 5 mins per block)
- Expert Mode scenarios (the 4-hour extended simulations)

**How it works:**
The Content Studio is built around the 13 Building Blocks and the 44 named format recipes. An instructor selects a format recipe (e.g., "Annotated Error Analysis" for PF2, or "Decision Simulation (6-node)" for PF3), fills in the clinical content and configuration, and publishes. The BB engine handles the interaction logic — no coding required.

**Magic Write:** Describe the exercise or KBB you want. AI generates a first draft in seconds, pre-formatted for the selected building block. The instructor reviews, adjusts, and publishes. The pre-built content is the starting point. Magic Write is the fast lane to new content.

**Publish / Draft workflow:** Content is never visible to learners until explicitly published. Drafts are accessible only to the creator and admins.

---

### 8.8 The 13 Exercise Building Blocks

Building Blocks are the core interaction mechanics. Each BB is a base engine that can be configured to produce multiple exercise format recipes — 44 named formats across 13 BBs.

| Code | Name | Interaction Type | Primary PF home |
|---|---|---|---|
| BB-01 | Tap to Mark | Tap a location on an image or word in a text | PF1, PF2 |
| BB-02 | Drag to Sort | Drag cards into category zones | PF1, PF2, PF4 |
| BB-03 | Drag to Order | Drag items into numbered sequence slots | PF1, PF2 |
| BB-04 | Drag to Match | Connect pairs / label image regions | PF1, PF2 |
| BB-05 | Drag to Position | Drag marker on a spectrum between two poles | PF1, PF4 |
| BB-06 | Multiple Choice | Select one or multiple options | PF1, PF2 |
| BB-07 | Scale Selector | Tap a point on a 5-point barometer | PF2, PF4 |
| BB-08 | Free Text Entry | Open-ended writing; AI evaluates against criteria | PF2, PF4 |
| BB-09 | Peer Write → Read → React | 3-step gated: write position → read peers → react | PF2, PF4 |
| BB-10 | Sequential Multi-Step Text | Text fields revealed step by step; factual → reflective | PF2, PF4 |
| BB-11 | Branching Decision Simulation | 6-node scenario with persistent risk bar | PF3 |
| BB-12 | Word Chip Assembly | Listen then assemble chips in correct order | Language Garden (SG) |
| BB-13 | Audio Record & Playback | Listen to model → record own voice → compare | Language Garden (SG) |

**The format recipe system:** Each BB powers multiple named formats. BB-01 (Tap to Mark) powers Hotspot Marking (PF1), Label-It (PF1), Annotated Error Analysis (PF2), and Listen & Identify (Language Garden). The building block is one mechanic; the recipe specifies the clinical content, configuration, and PF context.

**Total library:** 13 Building Blocks → 44 named exercise format recipes across PF1, PF2, PF3, PF4, and Language Garden.

### 8.10 Gamification & Motivation

**XP (Experience Points)**
Earned for completing PFs, KBBs, Language Garden exercises, and maintaining streaks. Never given for just logging in. Maps to Level progression (Level 1: "Curious Beginner" → Level 7: "Care Specialist" and beyond).

**Streaks**
Consecutive days with completed learning activity. Visible to learners on their dashboard, and visible to instructors as an early-intervention signal (streak break = possible disengagement).

**Achievements**
Milestone-based: "First KBB Complete", "7-Day Streak", "Language Star", "Module Master", "Clinical German B2". Unlockable through specific behaviours, not just time elapsed.

**Class Leaderboard**
Live XP ranking within the class. Refreshes with each activity. Ranks learning engagement, not test scores.

**The Lingo Otter 🦦**
Present at key moments — first login, module completion, Language Garden entry, long streaks. Also delivers adaptive referral suggestions in a warm, non-intrusive way. The human face of the AI layer.

## 9. The AI Layer — Lingo Intelligence

### The Two-Phase Architecture

**Phase 1 (Live):** API-orchestrated intelligence. Lingocare calls leading AI providers for generation, classification, and personalisation. The pedagogical framework, curriculum data, and learner context are passed as structured input to every AI call. The output is always grounded in the Lingocare pedagogical model — never generic.

**Phase 2 (In Development):** Proprietary Lingocare LLM — fine-tuned on the full corpus of Lingocare content: pedagogical framework, clinical German, nursing procedures, regional curriculum, and accumulated learner interaction data. Every cohort that uses Phase 1 generates training data for Phase 2. The more schools use Lingocare, the better the model becomes for every school.

### The Intelligence Stack

```
What the platform delivers
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Personalised Training  │  Content Engine  │  Expert Mode
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Lingocare Intelligence Layer
              (Ask Lingo · Adaptive Referral · Magic Write)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pedagogical  │  Clinical  │  Care        │  Regional
  Framework    │  German    │  Procedures  │  Curriculum
```

### Ask Lingo

Context-aware AI assistant present on all four client dashboards. Knows the curriculum, the class roster, the calendar, and the content library scoped to the user's role. Not a generic chatbot — a colleague that has read everything relevant to this user's world inside Lingocare.

### Magic Write

AI content drafting tool inside the Content Studio. Input: a topic description and target configuration (PF, language level, format recipe). Output: a first draft of the complete exercise or KBB, pre-formatted for the selected building block. The instructor reviews and publishes. Used to create new content and to adapt existing content for different language levels or class contexts.

### The Adaptive Referral Engine

Runs silently. Three triggers:

**Trigger 1:** Learner fails 2+ tasks of the same type → checks if Language Garden has relevant content → suggests it non-mandatorily.

**Trigger 2:** Learner set L1 or L2 at onboarding → Language Garden proactively recommended when entering a new Mega Cluster for the first time.

**Trigger 3:** Learner repeatedly struggles with a concept → relevant Knowledge Building Block surfaced proactively.

Lingo Otter delivers all referral suggestions in warm, conversational language. Referrals are never mandatory — learner agency is always preserved.

### Class-First AI

Every feature is tested in a real classroom before it ships. Every learner interaction trains the next version. This is not a marketing claim — it is an architectural principle. The pilot classroom data is the training signal for Phase 2. Every cohort improves the platform for the one that follows.

## 10. What We Deliberately Leave Out

**We do not teach general German.** Lingocare is for clinical nursing German — the language of care documentation, patient communication, and team coordination. General language learning is not in scope.

**We do not replace classroom instruction.** Lingocare is a digital complement to — not a substitution for — face-to-face teaching. The instructor's professional judgement is always in the loop.

**We do not diagnose.** The AI will never suggest a medical diagnosis, recommend a clinical intervention, or present itself as a substitute for qualified medical supervision.

**We are not a generic LMS.** Everything is structured around the Module → Topic → Lesson → LU architecture. This constraint is not a limitation — it is the source of the platform's pedagogical coherence.

**We are not a hospital system.** Lingocare operates in the education phase of training, not in active clinical practice. Integration with hospital systems is a future consideration.

**Expert Mode is not in Phase 1 MVP.** The 4-hour scenario simulations are designed and planned, but they ship after the core LU/PF system is validated with real cohorts.

**Language Garden is not mandatory.** It is a parallel track — always available, often recommended, never forced. Learners at L3 can choose to engage or not. Learners at L1/L2 are actively guided toward it, but never blocked from LU content if they skip it.

**We do not yet cover all German nursing curricula.** The MVP is built on the Hessen Pflegefachassistenz curriculum. Bayern and NRW mappings are on the roadmap.

## Appendix: Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Mega Cluster** | Top-level competency domain (AD, KI, BD, LP) — maps to a dominant nursing speech act |
| **Topic (Themenfeld)** | A date-ranged grouping of Lessons within a Module Block |
| **Module (Block)** | A month-ranged grouping of Topics — Block 1, Block 2, etc. |
| **Learning Unit (LU)** | One clinical scenario + 4 Practice Focuses — the core unit of learning |
| **Lab Lesson** | Scheduled practical skill session — no PFs, no exercises, marks hands-on practice time |
| **Practice Focus (PF1–PF4)** | One of four cognitive depth levels within a Learning Unit (freely selectable) |
| **Knowledge Building Block (KBB)** | Card+Quiz sequence (up to 5 mins, 5–6 cards) — the theoretical layer, reusable across LUs |
| **Building Block (BB-01–BB-13)** | The 13 base exercise interaction mechanics used to build all exercise formats |
| **Exercise Format Recipe** | One of 44 named exercise types, built from BBs, mapped to specific PFs |
| **Language Garden (LG)** | Dedicated clinical German practice space — one per active Mega Cluster, L1/L2 by default |
| **Expert Mode (Scenario)** | 4-hour clinical simulation placed in final Blocks — the highest integration level |
| **Adaptive Referral** | System-generated suggestion connecting a learner to the right support at the right moment |
| **Ask Lingo** | AI assistant present on all four client dashboards — context-aware, curriculum-aware |
| **Magic Write** | AI content drafting tool inside the Content Studio |
| **Lingo Otter 🦦** | Platform mascot — delivers adaptive referrals, Language Garden suggestions, milestone moments |
| **Curriculum Dimensions** | Blockwise (default), Action Competence Clusters (AD/KI/BD/LP), Custom Tags |
| **L1 / L2 / L3** | Lingocare's three receptive language levels (low / intermediate / advanced) — set at onboarding |
| **Class-First AI** | Every feature tested in a real classroom before shipping — architectural principle, not marketing |
| **DSGVO** | German GDPR — all data on EU servers, all processing DSGVO-compliant |
| **Phase 1 / Phase 2** | API-orchestrated intelligence (live) vs. proprietary Lingocare LLM (in development) |
