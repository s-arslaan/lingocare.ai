## 1. The Instructor's Real Problems — JTBD

### The Two Core Jobs

Research with nursing instructors consistently surfaces two primary needs that Lingocare must deliver on.

**Primary Job — Teach, not manage:**
> *"I want to stand in front of my class fully prepared and focused on my students — not buried in admin, content creation, or paperwork."*

This is what instructors name first. They chose nursing education because they care about the people they are training. What consumes their week — finding materials, formatting exercises, tracking who submitted what, managing absence cover — is not why they became teachers. Lingocare's primary job for the instructor is simple: **give the preparation work back to AI so the teacher can give their attention to the people in the room.**

**Secondary Job — Know who needs help before it is too late:**
> *"I need to see my class clearly — not as a group average, but as individual people — so I can reach the ones who are about to slip away."*

By the time a student stops attending, the relationship is often already broken. Instructors need signals they can act on — early, named, and clear. Not a spreadsheet. Not an end-of-block report. A name, a signal, and one tap to reach them.

The following scenario-based jobs describe how these two core needs manifest in real teaching situations.

---

### Job 1: Prepare for tomorrow's class without spending the evening on it

**The problem:** Belinda teaches three different classes this week, each at a different point in the curriculum. On Tuesday evening she has 90 minutes before she needs to sleep. Building materials from scratch for the AD1 session tomorrow — finding appropriate exercises, writing a case scenario, formatting it — will take all 90 minutes. And she will do the same thing next week.

**What Lingocare does:** She opens the dashboard. Her next class is shown. She taps **Prepare with Lingo**. Ask Lingo opens — already knowing her class, the topic scheduled for tomorrow, where the class is in the curriculum, and what content exists for that topic. It generates a preparation brief and suggests the relevant PF set. If she wants something different, she can adjust it in the Content Studio in minutes, not hours.

---

### Job 2: Know what is happening in the room during a live exercise

**The problem:** She has launched PF2 and 28 students are working. She has no idea whether students are flying through it, stuck, or have not started. She is walking the room asking questions individually — which means she cannot see the class pattern while it is forming.

**What Lingocare does:** The Live Class Dashboard shows real-time completion status for every student. As answers are submitted, she sees the Answer Distribution — where the class agrees, where it is split, and where a specific wrong answer is appearing repeatedly. She sees the discussion worth having before the exercise ends. She stops asking individuals. She asks the class.

---

### Job 3: Spot a student who is about to disappear — before they do

**The problem:** Amir was quiet last week. And the week before. He has submitted nothing in 15 days. Belinda has a vague sense of it but between three classes and everything else, it has not surfaced as a clear action.

**What Lingocare does:** The Alert panel names Amir. Not "Class 9B has 2 at-risk students." Amir Hasan — inactive for 15 days — Class 9B. One tap sends him a message through the platform, via Lingo, in a warm tone she has approved. She has acted in 30 seconds.

---

### Job 4: Build a custom exercise without starting from blank

**The problem:** Her school has a specific case scenario they always use for the MRSA isolation unit — it is their tradition, rooted in something that actually happened on a local ward. The platform's pre-built PF3 content is good, but she wants this specific case. Building a PF3 Decision Simulation from scratch sounds like a full day of work.

**What Lingocare does:** She opens the Content Studio. She selects BB-11 (Branching Decision Simulation). She types her case scenario description into Magic Write. In 45 seconds, a first-draft 6-node simulation appears — the case she described, the decision tree structured correctly, the risk bar logic mapped. She reviews it, adjusts two nodes, and publishes. Total time: 12 minutes.

---

### Job 5: Cover a colleague's class without preparing it from scratch

**The problem:** Frau Schmidt is ill. Belinda gets the message at 7:50am. The class starts at 9:00. The topic is Module 3 — a block she has not taught this year. She has 70 minutes.

**What Lingocare does:** She opens Ask Lingo and types: *"I need to cover Frau Schmidt's Module 3 class at 9am. What does Class 10C need today?"* Within seconds she has the topic, the scheduled Learning Unit, the pre-built PF1 exercise set ready to launch, and a brief on where the class is in the curriculum. She walks into the room knowing exactly what to do. The pre-built content was there. The AI joined the dots.

---

### Job 6: Understand why a class keeps getting stuck on the same concept

**The problem:** Every cohort struggles with the same AD2 exercise — the one on differentiating observation from interpretation. The class always splits on it. She has explained it three different ways. She does not know which learners understand and which do not.

**What Lingocare does:** The Answer Distribution view shows her exactly which answers her students chose, per task, per sub-task, across the whole class. The "Need Class Discussion" flag appears automatically when answer patterns show genuine conceptual disagreement — not just one wrong answer, but a meaningful split. She can see who chose what. She builds her next session around the specific moment of confusion, with names and answers to reference.

---

### Job 7: Give learners something to feel personalised — even in a class of 28

**The problem:** Her class has 12 international trainees with A2–B1 German, 8 German speakers who do not need language support, and 8 more in between. She cannot teach to all three of these groups simultaneously with one set of materials and no differentiation built in.

**What Lingocare does:** Every PF exercise adapts to each learner's language level automatically — the same content, in the language complexity that is right for each person. She does not need to create three versions. She does not need to manage differentiation manually. The platform does it. Every learner is reading the clinical case and completing the exercises in their own register — and she is seeing one unified class dashboard.

---

### Job 8: Add a personal note or resource to a lesson that appears exactly when learners need it

**The problem:** She found an excellent short video about hand disinfection technique from the Robert Koch Institut. She wants her students to see it when they open the AD1 LU on Hygienic Preparation — not via a WhatsApp link three days before, not via email she has to send. Just there, in the right place, at the right time.

**What Lingocare does:** From the Lesson detail view, she adds the video link as an attached resource. When any learner opens that LU during an active session, a small material pane appears in the exercise sidebar: "Extra from Belinda — RKI Hand Disinfection video." It is always there for that lesson. She does not send it. It arrives.

## 3. This Is Your Teaching Environment — Not an Admin Console

This distinction shapes everything about how the instructor experience is designed.

**What the school administration has set up:**
- The curriculum structure — Blocks, Topics, Lessons, LU assignments
- Class rosters — which students are in which classes
- Instructor assignments — which Topics belong to which teacher
- Term dates and scheduling

**What the instructor owns inside Lingocare:**
- Class preparation — when to run which PF, with what content
- Content creation and editing — inside the Content Studio
- Lesson materials and notes — added per lesson, visible to learners during class
- Learner relationships — one-click check-ins, personalised messages via Lingo
- Live class control — launching, monitoring, and closing a PF session
- Dashboard layout — what they see first, in what order

The sidebar and navigation reflect this split. The curriculum skeleton comes from the school. Everything the instructor does within it — preparing it, enriching it, monitoring it — is entirely theirs.

**Ask Lingo holds the whole thing together.** It knows the curriculum, the class rosters, the calendar, and the full content library. The instructor never has to navigate between systems to find what they need. Ask Lingo is the connective tissue across every piece of the instructor experience.

## 5. Lingo for Instructors — Ask Lingo in a Teacher's World

For instructors, Lingo is not a mascot or a motivational presence — it is a **working colleague**. The relationship is professional and warm, not playful. Lingo knows the instructor's curriculum, their class roster, their calendar, and the content library. It has done the reading. It can answer a question, draft something, or prepare a brief before the instructor has to ask twice.

### How Ask Lingo Communicates with Instructors

The tone is different from how Lingo communicates with learners. Lingo's instructor voice is:

**Efficient and direct.** The instructor is busy. Ask Lingo gets to the point. A preparation brief is four lines, not four paragraphs.

**Contextually primed.** Lingo speaks as if it has already checked the calendar, the class roster, and the content library. It does not ask the instructor to provide context it already has.

**Action-oriented.** Every response ends with the next concrete step: *"Here is what Class 9B needs today — want me to prepare the materials?"* Not just information. A proposed action.

**Honest about limits.** When the answer involves something outside Lingocare's scope, Lingo says so briefly and suggests what to do instead.

---

### What Ask Lingo Knows for Instructors

Ask Lingo on the instructor dashboard has access to:
- The instructor's full class schedule, including upcoming sessions pulled from the connected calendar
- The curriculum structure — which Topics and LUs are assigned, in what order, and how far each class has progressed
- The full content library — all pre-built PFs, KBBs, and Language Garden exercises, all accessible and searchable
- Individual learner data for every student in the instructor's assigned classes — performance, engagement, language level, last activity
- The Content Studio — Ask Lingo can initiate a Magic Write draft from a conversation

---

**Decision Score vs Activation Score:** Visible per-PF for PF2 and PF3 exercises. Decision Score reflects the quality of the learner's clinical reasoning — did they make the right choices and in the right sequence. A learner with a low Decision Score but high Activation Score is working hard but getting stuck — likely a conceptual gap, not a motivation issue. A low score on both signals something else.

**The instructor's add-note feature:** The instructor can add a private note to any learner profile — visible only to them, not to the learner. *"Spoken to Fatima after class — she mentioned language anxiety in clinical placement. Check-in scheduled for next week."* Notes are timestamped and accumulated across the term.

When a class session is live, the instructor has a dedicated real-time dashboard showing what is happening in the room — submission by submission, answer by answer, student by student.

**How to launch:**

From the dashboard Today card or calendar event → **Launch PF2 →** → Live Class Mode activates.

Learners see a notification: *"Class 9B · PF2 · AD1 · Belinda Kleinhans — Join?"* The session is live when at least one student joins.

**The Live Class Dashboard:**

**Three views available during a live session:**

### 6.6 Content Studio — The Instructor's USP

The Content Studio is where Lingocare's content-ready foundation becomes the instructor's creative environment. It is not a blank editor and it is not a rigid template system. It is a structured workspace where **the pre-built content is always the starting point, Magic Write is the fast lane, and the 13 Building Blocks are the vocabulary for anything custom.**

The Content Studio is accessible to Instructors and School Admins. Content created here belongs to the school and is visible to all instructors at the same school unless scoped otherwise.

#### Three modes of working in the Content Studio

**Mode 1 — Browse and assign existing content**

The entire pre-built content library is available to browse. Every PF for every LU, every KBB, and every Language Garden exercise. The instructor does not need to create anything — they select, preview, and assign.

### 6.8 Settings and Customisation

The instructor can configure their dashboard and experience to suit how they actually work.

**Dashboard layout:**
- Choose what to show first after Ask Lingo: My Classes, Today's Schedule, Alerts, or a combined view
- Toggle class progress bars on or off for each assigned class
- Set a default class (shows at the top of the My Classes tab)

**Ask Lingo communication preferences:**
- Response length: brief (3 lines max) or detailed (full briefing)
- Language: German or English for Ask Lingo responses
- Proactive morning briefing: on/off — whether Lingo sends a proactive summary when the instructor first opens the app

**Alert sensitivity:**
- Inactivity threshold: how many days of inactivity before a student appears in the red alert panel (default: 7 days; adjustable 3–14 days)
- Score threshold: the PF average score below which a student is flagged amber (default: 50%; adjustable)

**Notification settings:**
- Class start reminders (30 min before, 5 min before, or none)
- Alert notifications (immediate or daily digest)
- Content suggestion notifications from Lingo (on/off)
- Learner submission notifications (per class, per LU, or off)

**Lesson material defaults:**
- Whether the "From Belinda" materials pane is expanded or collapsed by default when learners open a PF during an active lesson

## 8. The Cross-Surface Principle

One rule behind all the navigation design: any meaningful piece of information is reachable from multiple starting points. The instructor should never be stuck thinking "where do I find this?"

**A pre-built PF exercise is reachable from:**
- Content Studio (browse library)
- Calendar (tapping a class event → View Materials)
- Dashboard (Today's schedule → View Materials)
- Ask Lingo ("What PF is scheduled for 9B tomorrow?")
- Curriculum View (tapping the LU)

**A student alert is reachable from:**
- Dashboard (Alerts panel — named)
- My Classes (Student Signals — named)
- Ask Lingo ("Who in 8A needs attention?")
- Learner Analytics (browsing that student's profile)
- Live Class dashboard (Schwache Leistung panel during a session)

**The Prepare with Lingo button appears on:**
- Dashboard (Today's schedule cards)
- Calendar (tapping any future class event)
- Curriculum View (each Lesson)
- Ask Lingo itself (as a suggested follow-up to any prep question)

This is not a convenience feature — it is the expression of the same underlying principle: **the instructor should never have to find their way around the platform.** The platform finds its way to the instructor.

**The Content Studio is reachable from:**
- Navigation sidebar (primary item)
- Dashboard Alerts panel (AI content suggestion → directly into the relevant exercise)
- Ask Lingo (typing "generate…" or "create…" opens Content Studio in Magic Write mode)
- Curriculum View (edit or attach any LU's content directly from the lesson)
- Live Class post-session summary (Act on content insights immediately)

**The floating Ask Lingo button means:**
No instructor ever navigates away to get help, generate content, or understand a data point. The question happens where they are. The result lands where they are. Then they keep working.
