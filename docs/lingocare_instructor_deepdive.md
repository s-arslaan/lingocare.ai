## 1. The Instructor's Real Problems — JTBD

Lingocare's single most important promise to the instructor: **it noticeably and meaningfully relieves them — before, during, and after every lesson.**

- **Before class:** Lesson preparation is faster, more targeted, and never starts from zero. Lingocare generates or surfaces materials so the instructor enters the room knowing exactly what to teach and who needs attention.
- **During class:** The instructor focuses on teaching and human connection. Lingocare handles the real-time tracking, the confusion detection, and the discussion prompts — so the teacher does not constantly diagnose and adapt alone.
- **After class:** Follow-up, correction, and documentation burden is reduced. The post-session summary flags what matters, not everything. There is less administrative tail after each lesson.

Relief is not a background benefit. It is the core design principle behind every screen, every workflow, and every AI interaction described in this document.

---

This relief is particularly critical because of a structural reality in nursing education: **classrooms are highly heterogeneous.** Students come with different German proficiency levels, different prior clinical knowledge, and different learning speeds. Instructors cannot teach to the middle without leaving faster learners under-stimulated and struggling learners behind. No one is truly served well.

**Lingocare AI's personalization is the direct answer to classroom heterogeneity.** Because the platform works with each trainee individually — at their exact level, in their language, at their pace — it absorbs the differentiation that would otherwise land on the instructor. The instructor no longer has to try to be five different teachers at once.


### Job 1: Prepare for today's class without losing 2–3 hours every time

**The problem:** Every lesson requires researching the topic, writing exercises, formatting, and uploading. For an instructor with three classes and four sessions per week, that adds up to 12+ hours of preparation — before any actual teaching happens.

**What Lingocare does:** The content is ready. All exercises, Knowledge Building Blocks (KBBs), and Practice Focus (PF) sets are available from the moment a school is onboarded — aligned to their *Lehrplan* (curriculum), ready to teach. For anything beyond the default, Magic Write generates a first draft in seconds. And when a class is 40 minutes away, one tap of the "Prepare" button opens Ask Lingo in Class Prep Mode — briefing the instructor on that class's completion status, which learners need attention today, what content is ready, and which discussion angles work for this PF level.

---

### Job 2: Know in real time what is happening in the class — not just after the fact

**The problem:** During a traditional classroom exercise, the instructor collects results through hand-raising or verbal response and gets an incomplete, delayed picture. Who actually understood? Where is the entire class confused on the same point?

**What Lingocare does:** The real-time teacher dashboard shows every learner's submission as it is made. The *Klassenübersicht* (Class Overview) bar chart updates live. When multiple learners choose the same wrong answer, the AI flags it immediately: "Confusion detected in Exercise 2 — 9 learners chose B, 6 chose C. Class discussion recommended." The instructor sees the class's confusion in real time, while they can still act on it.

---

### Job 3: Spot the learner who is about to drop out — before it is too late

**The problem:** By the time a learner's absence becomes visible — in attendance registers, assignment gaps, failed assessments — the relationship is often already broken. The early signals are there weeks before (missed submissions, declining scores, reduced engagement), but no tool surfaces them.

**What Lingocare does:** The alert system names specific learners: inactive for N days, consistently below threshold, not submitted. One tap opens that learner's profile and the option to send a check-in message directly through the platform.

---

### Job 4: Run exercises that generate real class discussion — not just individual scores

**The problem:** Most digital exercises produce a score. A score tells the instructor what happened — but not why, and it does not generate a conversation. The most valuable part of a nursing exercise is the debrief.

**What Lingocare does:** The *Antwortverteilung* (Answer Distribution) view shows exactly which options learners chose and why the class split. *Paarweise Antworten* (Paired Peer Responses) shows how student pairs reasoned through the same problem differently. The AI recommends "Class discussion required" when it detects competing answers across the class — a flag that turns an analytics signal into a discussion agenda item.

---

### Job 5: Create new content without coding, formatting, or starting from scratch

**The problem:** No tool currently generates nursing-specific, curriculum-aligned exercises in the format required for classroom use. Instructors build from Word documents, create slides in PowerPoint, and print materials that are not archived, trackable, or reusable.

**What Lingocare does:** The Studio gives instructors three creation paths: edit existing Lingocare content, create new content using format recipes and Magic Write, or build Expert Mode scenarios using the simulation builder. Every piece of content is automatically formatted for the learning system — no manual layout, no printing, no version control chaos.

---

### Job 6: Support international learners without running two parallel classes

**The problem:** A class with 30 learners might have 12 different mother tongues and four different German proficiency levels. What gets taught works for the majority — which means a significant share of the class follows at a fraction of comprehension.

**What Lingocare does:** Content is displayed at the learner's chosen language level (L1/L2/L3) automatically — the instructor does not prepare three versions of each exercise. The instructor can also enable word translation tooltips for their class, so learners see hover-translations in their mother tongue for clinical terms.

---

### Job 7: Cover for an absent colleague without losing your own preparation

**The problem:** When a colleague is absent, a senior instructor is asked to cover with 30 minutes' notice. Their own prep for the next day goes undone. This happens several times a term, and the compounding effect is real.

**What Lingocare does:** Every class in the system has its materials, LU/PF assignments, and learner history already in the platform. When an instructor covers for an absent colleague, they access that class's *Lehrplan*, the scheduled PF, today's content, and the at-risk learner list in under two minutes. Ask Lingo can brief them on the class they are about to walk into.

---

### Job 8: Have all materials and notes ready the moment class starts — without switching tabs

**The problem:** Instructors often have materials across three or four different places: USB drive, shared folder, school intranet, personal email. Pulling the right document in front of a room of learners takes time and breaks the session's flow.

**What Lingocare does:** Instructors add materials (files, KBBs, notes, links) directly to specific *Unterrichtseinheiten* (lesson slots) in the *Lehrplan*. When that lesson's PF is active — whether in solo or live class mode — a Materials Panel is accessible from a single tap, without leaving the session view.

## 3. The Intelligent Companion — What This Platform Is for Instructors

Lingocare is not a collection of features sitting next to each other. For an instructor, it is a system that knows:

- **Their schedule:** which class is next, what is assigned, when it starts
- **Their learners:** who is active, who is struggling, who has completed which PFs
- **Their curriculum:** which blocks are on track, which lessons need attention
- **Their content:** what is published, what is in draft, what in the library suits the next class
- **The live session:** who has joined, what they are submitting, where the class is confused right now

All of this connects through Ask Lingo. The question "who in 8A needs attention?" is not a database query — it is a question Lingo answers by checking 8A's current block, each learner's PF completion rates, inactivity signals, language level against exercise difficulty, and recent submission quality. The answer is specific: names, signals, and a suggested action.

The instructor does not carry all of this in their head. Lingocare does. The instructor's job is to teach.


### Not a Replacement — A Complementary Partner

**Lingocare AI is not a replacement for teachers. It is a complementary partner.**

Teachers bring everything that AI fundamentally cannot: human presence, empathy, professional judgment, real relationship, and lived clinical experience. What they do in the classroom — and the moments before and after — is irreplaceable.

Lingocare AI handles the **scalable, repetitive, and cognitively heavy** layer: personalized content delivery, language scaffolding, progress tracking, and preparation support. This frees the instructor to focus fully on the human part of teaching — the part only they can do.

> *Lingocare AI does what scales. The instructor does what matters.*

### This Is Your Teaching Environment — Not an Admin Console

The platform draws a clear line between what the school administration sets up and what the instructor owns.

**What the school administration has set up:**
- The curriculum structure — Blocks, Topics, Lessons, LU assignments
- Class rosters — which students are in which classes
- Instructor assignments — which Topics belong to which teacher
- Term dates and scheduling

**What the instructor owns inside Lingocare:**
- Class preparation — when to run which PF, with what content
- Content creation and editing — inside the Content Studio
- Lesson materials and notes — added per lesson, visible to learners during class
- Learner relationships — one-click check-ins, personalised messages via Lingo
- Live class control — launching, monitoring, and closing a PF session
- Dashboard layout — what they see first, in what order

The curriculum skeleton comes from the school. Everything the instructor does within it — preparing it, enriching it, monitoring it — is entirely theirs.

### How Ask Lingo Communicates with Instructors

For instructors, Lingo is not a mascot or a motivational presence — it is a **working colleague**. The relationship is professional and warm, not playful. Lingo knows the curriculum, the class roster, the calendar, and the content library. It has done the reading. It can answer a question, draft something, or prepare a brief before the instructor has to ask twice.

The tone is different from how Lingo communicates with learners. Lingo's instructor voice is:

- **Efficient and direct.** The instructor is busy. A preparation brief is four lines, not four paragraphs.
- **Contextually primed.** Lingo speaks as if it has already checked the calendar, the class roster, and the content library. It does not ask the instructor to provide context it already has.
- **Action-oriented.** Every response ends with the next concrete step — not just information, a proposed action.
- **Honest about limits.** When the answer involves something outside Lingocare's scope, Lingo says so briefly and suggests what to do instead.

---

### What Ask Lingo Knows

Ask Lingo has access to:
- The instructor's full class schedule, including upcoming sessions
- The curriculum structure — which Topics and LUs are assigned, in what order, and how far each class has progressed
- The full content library — all pre-built PFs, KBBs, and Language Garden exercises
- Individual learner data for every student in the instructor's assigned classes — performance, engagement, language level, last activity
- The Content Studio — Ask Lingo can initiate a Magic Write draft from a conversation

### The Floating Ask Lingo Button

Every screen has the floating 🦦 icon in the bottom-right corner. Tapping it opens a compact chat panel without leaving the current screen. Ask Lingo is context-aware:

- Inside the Live Class dashboard: *"I can see 8A is live — do you need discussion prompts for the results coming in?"*
- Inside the Content Studio: *"Tell me what you want to build and I'll generate a first draft."*
- Viewing a learner profile: *"Want me to draft a check-in message for Amir based on what you are seeing?"*

Ask Lingo is not buried in the sidebar — it is the primary panel on Startseite and a persistent floating colleague on every other screen.

**The guided dropdown** is the primary entry point. All four options represent the core instructor actions. The free-text field is visible at all times — it is the escape hatch for power users and for questions that don't fit the presets. This interaction pattern is consistent across every area of the dashboard.

**Class cards (cross-class overview):** Below the Lingo panel, one lean card per assigned class. The Startseite is the only cross-class view in the entire dashboard — everything else drills into a selected class.

| Element on card | Description |
|---|---|
| Class name + last activity | e.g. "Klasse 9B · zuletzt aktiv: heute" |
| Curriculum status (traffic light) | 🟢 Im Plan / 🟡 Leicht hinterher / 🔴 Deutlich hinterher |
| One warning signal (if present) | e.g. "2 Lernende mit Rückstand" or "PF2 in LU3 macht Schwierigkeiten" |
| Next recommended action (AI-generated) | e.g. "Klasse B sollte diese Woche AD1.3 abschließen" |

**Deliberately NOT on the class cards:** individual scores, competency area (KB) breakdowns, or table-style detail data. These are visible only after drilling into a specific class.

When the instructor taps a class card, the view shifts to that class's detail — a persistent class selector appears in the top bar, and the remaining three areas (Lesson Prep, Class Details, Individual Learners) become modes within this selected-class context. The sidebar reflects the current class: *Lehrplan*, *Auswertungen*, and *Lernende* all scope to the selected class.


**Dashboard customisation:** Instructors configure which widgets appear on Startseite from *Einstellungen*: default layout (Lingo panel, class cards, alerts), optional additions (mini class progress bars, pinned class spotlight, content draft reminders). Widget order is drag-to-rearrange. The `Prepare` button and the Next Class card are always visible — they cannot be removed.

### 5.2 Kalender (Calendar)

The instructor's schedule and planning space.

**Three event types:**

*🟠 Scheduled class session* — PF sessions the instructor has set up, linked to a specific LU and PF. These generate the learner's "Join Class" notification. Shows: class name, LU/PF, room, duration. When the class is within one hour: a "Join" button appears on this event and on the Startseite.

*🔵 Deadline* — LU completion deadlines set for specific learner groups. Shows live completion rate.

*⬜ Personal task* — Instructor-created reminders: "Mark 8A PF4 submissions", "Review KBB draft". Not visible to learners.

*🌱 Pre-class language exercise reminder* — Auto-generated when assigned pre-class language exercises are not yet completed by the class. Shows class completion rate.

**Tapping a class session:** Opens a session detail panel — class, LU/PF, time, room, prerequisite completion rate (what percentage of learners finished PF1 before today's PF2), attached materials, `Prepare` shortcut, `Start Class` button (active within 5 minutes of session time).

*Overview tab:* Class name, current block/*Themenfeld*, progress bar, last 3 sessions with completion rate, next scheduled session with Prepare button, alert panel for at-risk learners in this class.

*Learners tab:* Full roster — name, language level (L1/L2/L3), last active, current block progress, streak, XP rank. Tap any learner → Learner Profile. Quick action per row: "Send message."

*Materials tab:* All materials attached to this class's lessons. Add file, add link, add KBB, add note. Visibility toggle: "Only me" / "Visible to enrolled learners."

### 5.6 Lernende (Learner Profiles)

For every learner the instructor teaches, a full profile is accessible. The individual level — "who is this person and where do they stand?"

**Profile header:**
Name, photo (if set), language level (L1/L2/L3), mother tongue, enrolled class(es), current block, last active, current streak, total XP, level name.

**Signal bar at the top:**
Colour-coded status: 🟢 On track · 🟡 Needs attention · 🔴 At risk

**Tabs:**

*Progress:* Block-by-block completion, PF breakdown per LU (four PF dots per Learning Unit), overall completion rate.

*Performance:* Task-level accuracy over time (by PF type: PF1/PF2/PF3/PF4), weak area tags (task types they consistently score low on), strong areas (where they outperform the class average).

*Engagement:* Daily activity log as a heatmap calendar, streak history, live session attendance, submission timing (do they submit early or only at the deadline?).

*Communication:* Message history with this learner through the platform. "Send message" input. Private instructor notes (not visible to the learner).


**Three viewing modes** when browsing learners:

*Mode 1 — Learners needing support (early warning system):* System-flagged automatically. Skipped tasks, low scores, low activity. Goal: no learner gets lost before it's too late. The instructor sees named alerts with specific reasons — not a spreadsheet, but actionable signals.

*Mode 2 — High-performing learners:* Also system-flagged. Consistently high scores (near 100%), fast completion across all PFs. Goal: targeted differentiation, avoiding under-challenge. This mode surfaces learners who may be ready for PF3 ahead of schedule or who would benefit from extension material — the system actively surfaces opportunity, not just risk.

*Mode 3 — Look up an individual learner:* Instructor manually selects a specific learner by name — e.g. before a 1:1 meeting or feedback conversation. Shows strengths (by KB and topic), development areas, activity pattern, and the learner's AI conversation profile.

**AI conversation profile:** A short AI-generated summary (1–2 sentences) to help the instructor prepare a conversation in a targeted way. e.g. *"Amara zeigt besondere Stärken in AD und KI, hat aber PF2 in BD bisher kaum bearbeitet. Ein guter Gesprächseinstieg wäre, ihre Stärken zu benennen und gemeinsam zu überlegen, was sie in BD noch erkunden möchte."* (Amara shows particular strengths in AD and KI, but has so far barely worked on PF2 in BD. A good conversation opener would be to name her strengths and explore together what she would still like to discover in BD.)


**Klassenübersicht — live bar chart:**
Real-time bar chart. One bar per learner: Decision Score (orange) vs Activation Score (light orange). Updates live as learners submit. Bars fill in as exercises are completed — the chart builds itself during the session.

**Antwortverteilung — live answer distribution:**
For each exercise in the current PF: which answer options learners are choosing (green = correct, red = incorrect), updating in real time. When divergence is detected: "⚠ Confusion between learner answers detected" flag appears automatically. "Class discussion recommended" recommendation appears with the specific exercise highlighted — the instructor can pause and address it without losing momentum.

**Paarweise Antworten — paired peer responses (PF2/PF4):**
When exercises involve paired or group work, all pairs are shown side-by-side. The instructor sees which groups have completed, which are in progress. Tap any pair to view their responses.

**Session controls:**
- `Pause exercise` — stops all learner timers simultaneously
- `Extend time +5 min` — adds time for slow-finishing learners
- `Message class` — floating notification to all joined learners
- `Message individual` — from the *Schwache Leistung* panel, tap any name for a private nudge
- `Close session` — ends the live connection

**Materials Panel during live class:**
A collapsible sidebar accessible during the session (tap panel icon → slides in without covering the dashboard): all materials attached to this lesson in *Lehrplan* (files, KBBs, links, notes). Add on the fly during class — materials appear on the learner's side immediately. Private instructor notes are visible only to the instructor in this panel.


### Phase 2 — Debrief (After a PF Is Complete)

When enough learners have finished a PF, the instructor transitions directly into debrief mode. All debrief screens are designed to be **shared via screen share** with the class — large text, minimal UI chrome, optimised for classroom projection.

**Debrief screens** (instructor chooses which to show):

| Screen | Description |
|---|---|
| **Heat map** | Which tasks did the class struggle with most? Visual overview of error distribution across all tasks. e.g. "Aufgabe 3 · 68% Fehler" |
| **Word cloud / answer distribution** | For open or sorting tasks — what answers did the class give? Where were they split? e.g. Grauzonenkarten-Verteilung |
| **Discussion prompts (AI-generated)** | Ready-made discussion starters the instructor can use directly. e.g. "Hier haben 60% anders entschieden — warum?" · "Ergänzt das mit eurer Praxiserfahrung." |
| **Content fixpoints** | Pedagogically significant moments set by the content author — shown regardless of class performance. e.g. "Amara handelt aus Empathie — und gefährdet dabei die Asepsis. Diskussion: Wie kann man Herrn Jansen beruhigen, ohne das sterile Feld zu gefährden?" |
| **Praxistransfer prompt** | If flagged by the content author: a reminder to practise the physical action in the room. e.g. "Nehmen Sie sich jetzt 5–10 Minuten: Führen Sie die Händedesinfektion gemeinsam durch." — always shown last, as a closing physical action |

**Fixed display order in debrief:**
1. Word cloud / answer distribution (if present) — start with what the class actually did
2. Grey zone / answer distribution flags (sorted by % "unsure", descending)
3. Low-accuracy flags (sorted by error rate, descending)
4. Content fixpoints (always shown)
5. Free discussion prompt (if present)
6. Praxistransfer prompt (if set) — always last

**Deliberate omission during live monitoring:** Individual scores are NOT visible in real time. This avoids a competitive atmosphere during the exercise. Scores and detailed breakdowns appear only after the session ends, in the debrief.

## 7. The Cross-Surface Principle

The same information is reachable from multiple surfaces. The instructor never has to guess where to find something.

**A learner's at-risk signal is visible from:**
- Startseite (alert card, max 3)
- Auswertungen (engagement tab — sorted by last active)
- Lernende (full profile with all signals)
- Live class (*Schwache Leistung* panel during a session)
- Ask Lingo (any question about "who needs attention")

**A piece of content is reachable from:**
- Inhaltsbibliothek (browse or search)
- Lehrplan (linked to a lesson — lesson sidebar)
- Ask Lingo (describe what you need — Lingo finds it or builds it)
- Materials Panel during a live session
- Studio (editing or creating)

**A class session is schedulable from:**
- Kalender (tap empty slot)
- Meine Klassen → Sessions tab
- Lehrplan → lesson sidebar → [Schedule session]
- Ask Lingo ("Schedule a PF2 session for Klasse 9B next Tuesday")

**Class preparation happens from:**
- Startseite Prepare button
- Kalender session detail → Prepare shortcut
- Ask Lingo directly ("Prepare me for Klasse 9B")

All three lead to the same Class Prep Mode in Ask Lingo.

**The Lehrplan lesson sidebar brings together:**
- All PFs (Lingocare default + school-customised)
- Linked KBBs
- Uploaded materials and notes
- Pre-class language exercises with live completion rate

Everything for one lesson, in one place, without navigating away.
